# Regulatory Landscape: Data Protection & Electronics

*Research date: 2026-02-24*
*For: Returna ApS*

---

## Overview

These regulations create a powerful compliance driver for enterprise ITAD adoption. Companies handling end-of-life devices must prove proper data destruction, chain-of-custody, and sustainable disposal.

---

## 1. GDPR — General Data Protection Regulation

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Status** | In force since May 2018 |
| **Scope** | All personal data processing in EU |
| **Relevance to ITAD** | **Critical** |

### Key Requirements
- Data controllers must ensure secure deletion when data is no longer needed
- Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten") — Article 17
- Data breach notification within 72 hours — Article 33
- Processors (including ITAD vendors) must provide "sufficient guarantees"

### ITAD Implications
- Enterprises need **certified data erasure** with audit trails
- Chain-of-custody documentation required
- ITAD vendors must be vetted as data processors
- Certificates of destruction/erasure are essential

### Penalties
- Up to €20M or 4% of global annual turnover (whichever is higher)

---

## 2. ESPR — Ecodesign for Sustainable Products Regulation

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Status** | In force since July 2024 |
| **Full compliance** | Phased 2026-2030 |
| **Electronics deadline** | 2028-2029 |

### Key Requirements
- Durability, repairability, and recyclability standards
- Digital Product Passport (DPP) requirement
- Restrictions on destroying unsold goods (large enterprises from 2026)
- Circularity requirements for product design

### ITAD Implications
- Products must be designed for easier refurbishment/recycling
- End-of-life data must be tracked via Digital Product Passport
- Enterprises must document what happens to retired devices
- Creates demand for professional ITAD services

---

## 3. DPP — Digital Product Passport

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Legal basis** | Part of ESPR |
| **Electronics timeline** | 2028-2029 |
| **Battery passport** | Already piloting |

### What It Is
Electronic record containing:
- Product origin and materials
- Repair and maintenance history
- Environmental footprint data
- End-of-life/disposal recommendations
- Recycling instructions

### ITAD Implications
- **Every device will have a digital record tracking its lifecycle**
- ITAD processors must update DPP at end-of-life
- Creates accountability for proper disposal
- Returna platform could integrate with DPP systems
- Competitive advantage for ITADs with DPP capability

## 4. DORA — Digital Operational Resilience Act

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Status** | In force since January 17, 2025 |
| **Scope** | Financial sector entities |
| **Focus** | ICT risk management |

### Key Requirements
- Comprehensive ICT risk management framework
- Resilience testing requirements
- Third-party (vendor) risk management
- Incident reporting within 4 hours (initial) / 72 hours (full)

### ITAD Implications
- Financial institutions must vet ITAD vendors as critical third-party providers
- Data destruction must be part of ICT risk framework
- Audit requirements for device disposal
- Higher standards for ITAD serving banks, insurance, investment firms

### Who Must Comply
- Banks, credit institutions
- Insurance/reinsurance companies
- Investment firms
- Payment institutions
- Crypto-asset service providers

---

## 5. CRA — Cyber Resilience Act

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Status** | Adopted 2024, full compliance 2027 |
| **Scope** | All products with digital elements |
| **Standards** | 41 harmonized standards in development |

### Key Requirements
- Security by design for all digital products
- Vulnerability handling processes
- Software updates for product lifetime
- CE marking for cybersecurity compliance
- Incident reporting for vulnerabilities

### ITAD Implications
- Used devices must have data securely wiped to prevent vulnerability exposure
- End-of-life devices may still contain security risks
- Proper disposition prevents devices from becoming attack vectors
- Enterprises responsible for security throughout device lifecycle

### Product Categories
- **Default:** All digital products
- **Important:** Network devices, OS, identity management, routers
- **Critical:** Hardware security modules, smart meters, industrial automation

---

## 6. WEEE Directive — Waste Electrical and Electronic Equipment

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Status** | In force (updated regularly) |
| **Current version** | 2012/19/EU (recast) |
| **Collection targets** | 65% of equipment placed on market |

### Key Requirements
- Producers responsible for end-of-life collection and treatment
- Separate collection of e-waste
- Treatment standards for recycling facilities
- Reporting requirements for member states

### ITAD Implications
- Creates legal framework for e-waste handling
- Certified recycling required
- Producers must finance collection/treatment
- Creates market for professional ITAD services

---

## 7. NIS2 Directive — Network and Information Security

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Status** | In force since January 2023 |
| **Compliance deadline** | October 2024 |
| **Scope** | Essential and important entities |

### Relevance to ITAD
- Supply chain security requirements (Article 21)
- Entities must ensure security of ICT supply chain
- Includes device lifecycle management
- ITAD vendors are part of the supply chain

- Certified data destruction
- Proof of proper recycling/refurbishment
- Audit trails for regulators
