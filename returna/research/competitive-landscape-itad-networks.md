# Competitive Landscape: Global ITAD Networks & Platforms

*Research date: 2026-02-24*
*Author: Clorius (for Kasper Horn)*

---

## Executive Summary

The global ITAD market is dominated by **vertically integrated giants** who own their processing facilities worldwide. Returna's opportunity is to become the **orchestration layer** that enables independent ITADs to compete with these networks — without requiring them to build global infrastructure.

**Key insight:** The giants are potential competitors only if Returna tries to own facilities. But as a neutral platform, they could eventually become customers or partners.

## Returna's Competitive Advantages

### 1. Neutral Platform
- Giants own facilities → compete with local ITADs
- Returna doesn't process → no conflict of interest
- Enterprises get choice; ITADs get demand

### 2. Asset-Light Scaling
- Giants: $100M+ to build global network
- Returna: Software platform + partnerships
- Can scale coverage without capital expenditure

### 3. Return Hub Value Prop
- Small/mid ITADs can't compete with Ingram Micro alone
- Returna gives them enterprise access
- Network effect: more hubs → more coverage → more enterprise demand

### 4. Box Program Specialization
- Giants focus on bulk/office pickups
- Returna built for hybrid workforce (ship-to-employee)
- Box program expertise from Scandic IT operations

### 5. Transparent Economics
- 10% platform fee (vs opaque enterprise pricing from giants)
- 90% to Return Hub + 30% of resale
- Clear value for all parties

## Key Takeaways for Returna

1. **Don't try to compete head-to-head** with Ingram Micro, Sims, Iron Mountain
2. **Own the orchestration layer** — let them own facilities
3. **Target the mid-market ITADs** (20-150 employees) who can't compete alone
4. **ServiceNow integration is table stakes** — all major players have it
5. **Box programs are underserved** — giants focus on bulk, not employee-level
6. **Neutrality is the moat** — enterprises want choice, not vendor lock-in

---

## Appendix: Arrow Exit (2019)

Arrow Electronics exited the PC/mobility ITAD business in 2019, creating market disruption. This shows that even major players struggle with the complexity of global ITAD. Returna's asset-light model avoids this trap.
