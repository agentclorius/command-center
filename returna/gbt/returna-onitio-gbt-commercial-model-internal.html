GBT Commercial Model

Internal view of how Returna should structure the customer relationship, operating model, and economics with Onitio and Return Hubs.

Returna — Internal

WORKING DRAFT

Returna should own the customer contract and commercial layer. Onitio should deliver field service. Return Hubs should support parts, logistics, depot repair, and recovery only when used.

Returna

  • Owns customer relationship and pricing
  • Defines service scope and exclusions
  • Orchestrates all delivery partners
  • Owns reporting and governance

Onitio

  • Provides onsite field service execution
  • Handles incident-based repair activity
  • Is paid per approved service event

Return Hubs

  • Supply approved spare parts
  • Support handling and dispatch
  • Perform depot repair and recovery
  • Support reverse logistics where relevant
Why this structure works

Commercial logic

  • Keeps Returna in control of the enterprise relationship
  • Avoids thin reseller positioning
  • Supports recurring annual revenue
  • Gives GBT one commercial interface

Strategic logic

  • Proves Returna can orchestrate lifecycle support, not just end-of-life
  • Gives Return Hubs a meaningful role beyond collection only
  • Creates a reusable enterprise support model

Payment logic

  • GBT pays Returna annual pooled fee per covered device
  • Onitio is paid per incident
  • Hubs are paid only when they contribute actual value
  • No default passive revenue share to hubs

Controls required

  • Device eligibility rules
  • Clear claim scope and exclusions
  • Parts caps and approval thresholds
  • Onsite vs depot routing logic

Bottom line: This should be positioned as a Returna-managed lifecycle support / reuse support service for out-of-warranty devices, not a simple resale of an Onitio service. The model is commercially attractive if utilization is controlled and scope is defined tightly before external pricing.
