## Key Regulations Driving Demand

### 🇪🇺 EU Regulations (Active 2024-2027)

| Regulation | Effective | Impact on Enterprise IT |
|------------|-----------|------------------------|
| **CSRD** | 2024-2026 | Mandatory ESG reporting incl. circular economy metrics |
| **NIS2** | Oct 2024 | Supply chain security, audit trails for IT assets |
| **ESPR** | Jan 2025 | Ecodesign requirements, ban on destroying unsold electronics |
| **Digital Product Passport** | 2026-27 | Lifecycle data required for electronics |
| **Right to Repair** | July 2026 | Spare parts & documentation mandated |
| **WEEE (strengthened)** | Ongoing | Extended producer responsibility |

### 🇺🇸 US Regulations

| Regulation | Effective | Impact |
|------------|-----------|--------|
| **FTC Safeguards Rule (GLBA)** | Updated 2024 | Financial institutions: secure disposal of customer data |
| **HIPAA** | Active | Healthcare data: documented destruction required |
| **CCPA/CPRA** | 2023+ | California: consumer rights incl. deletion |
| **SEC Climate Disclosure** | Pending | Scope 3 includes IT asset lifecycle |

### 🔒 Data Security (Global)

| Standard | Scope | Requirement |
|----------|-------|-------------|
| **GDPR Art. 17** | EU + global | Documented data erasure |
| **NIST 800-88** | US Federal | Data sanitization standard |

## Why This Matters Now

### The Problem Enterprises Face

1. **CSRD requires reporting** on circular economy practices — but most enterprises don't track device end-of-life
2. **GDPR mandates documented erasure** — 4% of global revenue at risk for non-compliance
3. **ESPR bans destruction** of functional electronics — enterprises need certified refurb/resale channels
4. **Digital Product Passports** require lifecycle data — enterprises lack the infrastructure

### The Gap in the Market

- **250M+ corporate devices** leave company control annually
- No global platform standardizes end-of-life workflow
- Enterprises rely on fragmented, local ITAD vendors
- Audit trails are manual, incomplete, or non-existent
