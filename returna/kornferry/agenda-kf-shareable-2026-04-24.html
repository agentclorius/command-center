Contract mechanics — shared overview for Kornferry

Kornferry × RDT on the Returna platform

Prepared 2026-04-23 · management discussion 2026-04-24

The structure in one paragraph: Kornferry contracts with RDT — already their trusted office-ITAD partner, ISO 27001-certified. RDT is the invoicing entity and the name on the SOW. KF uses the Returna platform from day 1 — portal, reporting, ITSM integration, multi-region orchestration — with Returna named as a sub-processor in the DPA.

Physical execution by region: UK → RDT's own facility. EU, NA, APAC, LATAM → verified Return Hubs in the Returna network. Each Hub operates under its own certifications for its region; RDT's ISO anchors the contractual chain.

Phase 2 (at renewal, planned): once Returna is ISO 27001 certified, the contract can be moved from RDT to Returna. RDT's role shifts to ambassador — relationship maintainer, rev share, introducer for future deals. KF will be able to use more platform features once invoiced directly through Returna.

“You contract with RDT. You use Returna. Physical work is done by the right local Hub in each region.”
From the existing draft Suggested addition

1 · Contract structure

  • Contract based on RFP; SOW signed by both parties
  • 15-month minimum term: 12-month lock-in + 3-month notice (RDT ROI)
  • If KF doesn't cancel, yearly review 3 months before renewal — SOW + pricing adjustment
  • SLA penalties in place
  • Formal change request process
  • Contracting entity — which KF entity signs, which RDT entity signs
  • Governing law — EU seat vs UK vs neutral
  • Right-to-publicise — logo use / case study / reference

2 · Commercial model

  • 60-day payment terms
  • Minimum spend commitment over 15 months
  • Pricing structure — service fee, per-device rate, volume tiers
  • Currency & FX — EUR / GBP / USD, who bears FX risk
  • Resale revenue share — split across KF, RDT, marketplace
  • Billing cadence — monthly vs quarterly; late-payment interest
  • True-up model — over/under minimum mechanics
  • Termination economics — early exit fee schedule

3 · Scope

  • Device categories — laptops ✓; confirm desktops / mobiles / monitors / servers / peripherals
  • Geographic coverage — UK via RDT; EU, NA / APAC / LATAM via verified Return Hubs in the Returna network
  • Services in scope — deployment, returns, in-life swap, image refresh, disposal, erasure cert, refurb+resell, redeployment
  • Out-of-scope — consumables, desk phones, anything KF handles elsewhere
  • Volume forecast — devices/month by region; seasonality

4 · SLA matrix

  • Deployment SLA — KF order → laptop in user's hands (by region)
  • Return / pickup SLA — ticket raised → collection scheduled
  • Processing SLA — receipt → erasure + disposition
  • Certificate SLA — cert produced within N business days
  • Exception SLA — hold times for damaged / missing devices
  • Reporting SLA — monthly by day N, quarterly by day N
  • Penalty mechanics — service credits % vs liquidated damages; cap per quarter

5 · Operational governance

  • Operational + service quarterly reviews
  • Onboarding plan — 30/60/90 day milestones, named contacts both sides
  • Monthly operational call — volumes, exceptions, roadmap
  • Quarterly business review — KPIs, strategic direction, pricing
  • Annual executive sponsor meeting — commercial / renewal
  • Tooling & reporting portal — Returna platform (ticketing, asset register, status, certificates, reporting)
  • Escalation path — operator / manager / exec on both sides

6 · Technical & integration

  • RDT permissions in KF's Intune environment
  • Image available upfront with RDT
  • Test plan understanding KF requirement
  • Intune / Autopilot enrolment — RDT on behalf of KF; Azure AD scope, least-privilege
  • ITSM integration — KF ITSM ↔ Returna platform ticketing
  • Image governance — owner, versioning, update cadence, sign-off
  • Encryption verification — every returned device's state confirmed & logged
  • Data wipe cert — aligned to NIST SP 800-88 Rev 2 and IEEE 2883-2022; PDF + API; serial-matched
  • Asset tag standard — barcoding, cross-ref to KF's register
  • API / webhook — Returna endpoints via RDT contract

7 · Data protection & compliance

  • DPA — GDPR + UK GDPR; KF controller, RDT processor
  • Sub-processor list — Returna (platform) + verified Return Hubs active at contract start + carriers + erasure SW + any remarketing partners; substitution-flexibility clause for Hubs of equivalent certification
  • In-region processing by design — Returna routes devices to Hubs within their region (EU stays in EU, UK in UK). UK ↔ EU covered by mutual adequacy. SCCs / IDTA / DPF in the DPA as a safety net for exceptional flows only
  • Security certifications — RDT's ISO 27001 anchors the contract; every verified Hub holds region-appropriate certifications under Returna's vetting standard; Returna ISO in progress (relevant at phase 2); erasure aligned to NIST SP 800-88 Rev 2 and IEEE 2883-2022
  • Breach notification — 24h target, 72h max; named contacts, template
  • Audit rights — annual + on-request; SOC 2 Type II may satisfy in lieu
  • Personnel vetting — background checks + NDA flow-down to facility staff
  • Record retention — certificates retained N years, retrievable

8 · Risk, insurance & liability

  • Insurance cover for goods and warehouses
  • Optional transit insurance for high-value items
  • Insurance limits & certificates — public liability, product, PI, cyber — KF's min limits
  • Liability cap — typical 12-month fees; carve-outs for data breach / IP / gross negligence
  • Indemnification — third-party claims, breach, IP, personal injury
  • Title & risk transfer — device title across KF / RDT / buyer
  • Business continuity — RDT site outage: backup capacity, RTO
  • Force majeure — scope, notification, mitigation

9 · Exit strategy

  • EU+UK zone → RDT: new + used equipment to RDT warehouse
  • Used equipment: inbound checklist to ensure laptop is working correctly
  • Transition period — 60/90/120 days post-termination at BAU pricing
  • Handover deliverables — final asset register, certs, images, history, open tickets, residual inventory
  • Return of KF data — within N days; secure deletion
  • Data / devices in-flight — what happens mid-transit, mid-processing, mid-resale
  • Residual stock — KF-owned inventory: return or dispose
  • Onward IP — KF keeps image/data/register; RDT keeps platform/tooling
  • Non-solicit — staff hiring limits for a period

Likely KF questions to prepare for

  • "Who actually touches our laptops?"RDT warehouse, named site, vetted staff.
  • "What happens if RDT's site goes down?"Backup capacity, BCP, alternate processing.
  • "How do we know the data is gone?"Cert aligned to NIST SP 800-88 Rev 2 and IEEE 2883-2022, serial-matched, API-retrievable.
  • "Can we audit?"Annual + on reasonable notice; SOC 2 Type II available.
  • "What's my exit?"3-month notice after month 12, clear handover deliverables.
  • "What am I locking into for 15 months?"Min spend, change request mechanism if scope shifts.
  • "Who's responsible if a device is lost?"Insurance, liability cap, breach process.
  • "Can we extend beyond EU+UK?"Yes — Returna orchestrates additional Hubs under the same framework.
  • "Who's the Hub in each region?"UK → RDT. EU / NA / APAC / LATAM → verified Return Hubs in the Returna network, each operating under region-appropriate certifications.
  • "What does my portal look like day-to-day?"Demo of the Returna platform — ticketing, asset register, certs, reporting.
  • "How much resale value comes back?"Split model, transparency on proceeds.
  • "Why RDT contract but Returna tools?"RDT has ISO & existing relationship. Returna is named sub-processor. Each Hub operates under its own certifications. Migration to Returna-as-prime available at renewal once Returna is ISO-certified.
