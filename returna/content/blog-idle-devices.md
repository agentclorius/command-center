# The €1,000 Devices Collecting Dust in Your Drawers

*Why redeploying idle IT assets beats buying new — and how to do it at scale*

---

Every enterprise has them. Laptops in desk drawers. Phones in storage closets. Devices sitting with former employees who left months ago. IT assets that still work, still have value, but aren't doing anything.

These aren't broken devices. They're not obsolete. They're just... idle.

And while they sit there, your company is ordering brand new hardware at €1,000+ per unit.

## The hidden fleet nobody talks about

The numbers are staggering. According to research by Oomnitza and YouGov:

- **50% of companies** have lost at least 5% of their endpoint devices when employees leave
- Among large enterprises (10,000+ employees), **25% lose more than 10%** of devices at offboarding
- Most of these devices still contain corporate data

These aren't outliers. This is the norm. The combination of remote work, hybrid offices, and high employee turnover has created a massive pool of untracked, underutilised IT assets scattered across homes and offices worldwide.

## The math that changes everything

Let's look at what happens with a typical idle device:

**Option A: Traditional buyback**
- Device sitting in a drawer: €0 value today
- Send it to ITAD for buyback: €100-150

**Option B: Redeploy it**
- Cost to retrieve, wipe, and redeploy: €50-100
- New device you *don't* have to buy: €1,000-1,200

**The difference: 8-10x the value.**

For a company with 10,000 employees and a typical 3-4 year refresh cycle, hundreds of devices are swapped, upgraded, or left behind by leavers every year. If even 200 of those can be redeployed instead of replaced, you're looking at:

- **Traditional approach**: 200 × €125 buyback = **€25,000**
- **Redeployment approach**: 200 × €1,100 saved = **€220,000**

That's not a rounding error. That's a line item that matters.

## Why this matters more in 2026

If this math wasn't compelling enough, the market is making it urgent:

- **Laptop prices are rising 15-20%** this year, according to Lenovo and industry analysts
- **Component shortages** (CPUs, memory) are structural, not cyclical — expect them through 2027
- **Sub-€600 business laptops** are becoming nearly impossible to source
- **Lead times are lengthening**, making last-minute procurement painful

The days of "just order new ones" are getting expensive. Companies that can extend device lifecycles and redeploy existing assets have a real cost advantage.

## What's actually wrong with these devices?

Here's what we see with "idle" enterprise devices:

| Condition | Typical issue | Solution | Cost |
|-----------|---------------|----------|------|
| Data on device | Previous user's data still present | Certified data erasure | €25 |
| Needs reinstall | Old image, wrong config | Fresh OS deployment | €15 |
| Cosmetic damage | Scratched top cover, worn keyboard | Top cover skin + keyboard print | €50-100 |
| Minor repair | Battery degraded, sticky keys | Component replacement | €50-150 |
| Needs upgrade | Insufficient RAM or storage | Memory/SSD upgrade | €50-200 |

Most idle devices need nothing more than a wipe and a reinstall. Some need light refurbishment. Very few are actually end-of-life.

## The remote workforce problem

The challenge isn't identifying that idle devices exist — it's getting them back.

When employees worked in offices, device collection was straightforward. IT walked around, collected equipment, and processed it centrally.

With distributed workforces, that model is broken. Devices are:

- At former employees' home addresses
- In regional offices with no IT presence
- With contractors who finished their engagement
- In desk drawers at co-working spaces

Collecting these devices requires:
- Global logistics coordination
- Local pickup capabilities
- Secure chain of custody
- Certified data handling

## The sustainability angle (that's also good business)

Let's be clear: redeploying devices isn't just about saving money. It's about not throwing away perfectly good hardware.

Every laptop that gets a second life instead of heading to recycling:
- Avoids ~300kg of CO2 emissions from manufacturing a replacement
- Keeps valuable materials in circulation
- Reduces e-waste

But unlike most sustainability initiatives, this one *saves* money instead of costing it. You don't have to choose between environmental responsibility and financial performance.
