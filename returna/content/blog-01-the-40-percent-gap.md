# The 40% Gap: Why Europe Is Leaving Value on the Table

_Draft for Returna blog — February 2026_

---

In the US, over 40% of devices find their way back into the circular economy through trade-in programs. In parts of Europe? Just 10-20%.

That's not a rounding error. That's billions of euros in unrealized value, sitting in desk drawers and landfills across the continent.

## The Numbers Don't Lie

At this week's CCS Insight Circular Markets event in London, one data point stood out above the rest: the stark gap between US and European take-back rates.

American carriers, retailers, and OEMs have turned device trade-in into a strategic lever. "Guaranteed value" and "future value" offers aren't sustainability initiatives — they're competitive weapons.

Meanwhile, European enterprises and consumers are still treating end-of-life devices as an afterthought.

## Why the Gap Exists

The difference isn't consumer intent. Europeans care about sustainability as much as anyone — arguably more. The gap comes down to three factors:

**1. Fragmentation**

The US market is consolidated. A handful of carriers dominate. Europe? Dozens of operators across 27+ markets, each with different regulations, languages, and logistics networks.

**2. Complexity**

For enterprises, returning devices from a distributed workforce across multiple countries is operationally painful. Without a unified platform, most don't bother.

**3. Incentive Design**

US programs have mastered the art of making trade-in frictionless and financially attractive. European programs are often an afterthought — bolted on rather than built in.

## The Opportunity

Here's the thing: that gap is an opportunity, not a condemnation.

The infrastructure exists. The demand exists. What's missing is the orchestration layer — a platform that makes circular execution as simple as linear disposal.

When European enterprises can return devices from employees in Stockholm, Madrid, and Warsaw with the same ease as shipping to a single warehouse, participation will follow.

When Return Hubs across the continent can receive standardized volume without building their own enterprise sales teams, supply will scale.

The 40% gap won't close through awareness campaigns. It'll close through operational excellence.

## The Path Forward

At Returna, we believe the future of circular IT isn't about convincing people to care — they already do. It's about removing the friction that prevents participation.

That means:
- **Unified orchestration** across geographies
- **Standardized workflows** that work for enterprises of any size
- **Strong partner economics** that make Return Hubs eager to participate

The US figured this out. Europe's turn is coming.
