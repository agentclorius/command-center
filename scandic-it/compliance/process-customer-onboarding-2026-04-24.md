## 1. Purpose

This process describes how a new customer is evaluated, contracted, and brought into live service at Scandic IT. It ensures that every customer engagement starts with clear commercial, legal, and operational foundations — and that information security and data protection requirements are agreed up front.

## 2. Scope

All new customers engaging Scandic IT for ITAD services, from first qualified enquiry to first completed collection.

## 4. Process flow

### Step 1 — Qualified enquiry
- Enquiry captured (email, referral, direct)
- Initial needs: device types, expected volume, geographic scope, urgency
- Go/no-go decision based on fit with Scandic IT's scope and capacity
- **Output:** qualified lead record

### Step 2 — Commercial scoping
- Discovery conversation on: volumes, cadence, device mix, disposition preferences (resell / recycle / return)
- Special requirements identified: customer-specific erasure standards, reporting format, ITSM integration
- **Output:** initial scope and pricing offer

### Step 3 — Due diligence (both ways)
- Scandic IT's compliance evidence shared: ISO 27001 status, certifications, insurance certificates, relevant policies in their light / shareable version (full policies only after NDA)
- Customer's security questionnaire completed if requested
- Customer's own compliance position reviewed (especially for data-subject protection)
- **Output:** exchanged compliance evidence

### Step 4 — NDA
- Mutual NDA signed before exchanging full policies or sensitive operational detail
- NDA record retained per Data Classification Policy
- **Output:** signed NDA

### Step 5 — Master services agreement / customer cooperation agreement
- Commercial contract signed covering scope, SLAs, pricing, term, termination, liability, insurance
- Template maintained by Kasper; legal review on material deviations
- **Output:** signed MSA

### Step 6 — Data Processing Agreement (DPA)
- DPA signed if the engagement involves personal data (almost always for ITAD)
- Sub-processor list disclosed (Montes, Certus, Piceasoft, carriers, recyclers)
- International transfer mechanisms covered (SCCs / UK IDTA / DPF as needed)
- **Refers to:** Supplier & Third-Party Security Policy
- **Output:** signed DPA

### Step 7 — SOW / engagement setup
- Statement of Work signed covering specific activities, pricing schedule, start date
- Account setup: primary POC, invoicing details, ITSM integration if applicable
- Customer portal access provisioned (if applicable)
- **Output:** signed SOW, account configured

### Step 8 — Operational handoff
- Kickoff call with customer, operations, and account manager
- First pickup scheduled
- Chain-of-custody form and packaging guidance shared
- Customer briefed on reporting format and cadence
- **Refers to:** Device Handling — Full-Circle Process
- **Output:** operational readiness confirmation

### Step 9 — First collection
- Executed per the Device Handling process
- First job reviewed end-to-end by operations lead
- First customer report reviewed by Kasper before dispatch
- **Output:** first completed job + customer report

### Step 10 — Customer confirmation & live status
- Customer confirms satisfaction with first engagement
- Any refinements applied (reporting format, cadence, specific handling)
- Customer moves to "live" status in commercial system
- First annual customer satisfaction survey scheduled (updated from quarterly in response to Stage 1 audit 2026-04-24)
- **Output:** customer marked live; ongoing service cycle begins

## 6. Policy awareness during onboarding

Before any customer-facing staff start work on the account, they must acknowledge:
- Applicable policies (Acceptable Use, Data Classification, Physical Security, Access Control)
- The specific customer's DPA sub-processor constraints
- Any special data-handling requirements

Awareness recorded per Security Awareness Training Record.
