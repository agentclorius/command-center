Insert for QMS Manual — "Opportunities"

To be added to: Quality Management System Manual

Suggested section: within Clause 6.1 (Actions to address risks and opportunities) — typically alongside the existing risk paragraph

Rationale: ISO 9001:2015 Clause 6.1 explicitly requires the organization to determine BOTH risks AND opportunities. Closes a gap flagged in Stage 1 audit feedback 2026-04-24.

X.X Opportunities

Scandic IT identifies and acts on opportunities as part of the same management cycle used for risk. Where risks are threats that could prevent the QMS from achieving its intended results, opportunities are favourable conditions or ideas that could enhance the QMS, improve service quality, increase efficiency, strengthen customer relationships, or reduce long-term cost or exposure.

Sources of opportunities

Opportunities are identified from any of the following:

  • Management review — strategic review meetings surface opportunities from leadership
  • Customer feedback — surveys, direct enquiries, complaint trends, and contract renewals
  • Internal audit findings and observations — Opportunities for Improvement (OFIs) raised during internal audit
  • External audit feedback — observations and recommendations from certification bodies and customer audits
  • Supplier and technology developments — new capabilities from existing suppliers (e.g. Certus, Piceasoft), new market offerings, new standards
  • Regulatory and industry changes — new requirements or voluntary frameworks
  • Employee suggestions — operational staff often see efficiency opportunities first
  • Incident and non-conformity analysis — lessons learned that suggest systemic improvement

Analysis and evaluation of opportunities

Each identified opportunity is analysed against the same dimensions used for risk, plus opportunity-specific criteria. This keeps risk and opportunity analysis symmetric inside the Clause 6.1 planning cycle.

Dimension Question
Value What positive impact would this have on service quality, customer satisfaction, efficiency, compliance, risk reduction, or revenue?
Feasibility Cost, complexity, resources needed, time to implement?
Fit with strategy and scope Does this align with Scandic IT's management-system scope and business direction?
Upside if taken Quantitative or qualitative benefit if the opportunity is successfully acted on
Downside if ignored What do we lose or miss if we do not pursue this?
Dependencies Standalone, or dependent on another initiative being completed first?

Opportunities are prioritised on a simple High / Medium / Low scale and assigned an owner with a target date for the first concrete step.

Example analysis — O-01 "Service development to attract additional customers":

  • Value: broaden customer base, reduce dependence on existing customers, increase resilience of the business
  • Feasibility: medium — requires service design, pricing, and pilot customers. Can be phased.
  • Fit: directly aligned with Scandic IT's scope (ITAD services at Tarmvej 8) and does not expand the certified scope in a way that would invalidate the certification
  • Upside if taken: more revenue streams, lower customer-concentration risk, improved negotiation position with suppliers
  • Downside if ignored: stagnating customer base, reliance on fewer accounts, slower growth
  • Dependencies: market scan + pricing model required before pilot
  • Priority: High
  • First step: scope-out workshop scheduled by Kasper

Tracking and action

Accepted opportunities are recorded in the Improvement Plan (see Management Review Minutes and Corrective Action Log). Each active opportunity has:

  • Description
  • Expected benefit
  • Owner
  • Target date (where appropriate)
  • Status

Progress is reviewed at every management review (quarterly).

Review

Opportunities are reviewed and updated at least quarterly during management review, and whenever new sources (audit findings, customer feedback, incidents) surface additional items.
